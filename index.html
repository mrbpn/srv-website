Principles

01 · Principles

Working rules

  • The grid shows — equal cells, strong rules, visible structure.
  • Everything is Archivo, flush left; weight and scale do the organising.
  • One red accent, used sparingly — the page is ink on near-white.
01 · Principles

Two columns

Two equal cells

The content width halves into two equal cells on a one-unit gutter. Copy sets flush left and ragged right — the grid does the measuring, and the rag stays honest.

A rule opens each column

Each column starts under a 2px rule, the header band's weight carried down the page. Both columns top-align and share the 48-pixel rhythm, line for line.

01 · Principles

Three columns

A brief measure

Three cells bring the measure near thirty characters — short-copy territory. Keep paragraphs to a few lines and let the cells stay even.

The rag works harder

At this width the rag shows every decision. Break lines by sense where a break matters; the inherited pretty wrapping handles the rest.

Figures stay tabular

Columns of numbers keep their tabular forms at any measure, so digits align down the cell the way the kicker and the table already set them.

Imagery

03 · Imagery

The grayscale treatment

Every photograph prints in pure black and white — the grayscale wrapper keeps imagery architectural, and the red stays reserved for marks.

03 · Imagery

Full bleed

One photograph, edge to edge — the scrim is the ground token rising from the foot, so the type keeps its contrast without leaving the palette.

03 · Imagery

Half bleed

  • The photograph bleeds to three edges and holds half the stage
  • The copy keeps the content rhythm — same gridlines as every slide
  • Marks still hang left of the text, whatever sits behind them
03 · Imagery

Mirrored

The flip class changes the photograph's side and the copy returns to the text axis — one pattern, both directions.

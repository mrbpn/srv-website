Academics

A strong foundation, from the first day of school.

From age 2 through Grade 8, teaching follows the national curriculum in English medium, delivered with interactive technology and participatory learning in every classroom.

PG · Nursery · LKG · UKG

Early Years

Our Pitter Patter World curriculum takes children from 18 months onward through a play-based, Montessori-informed programme that builds language, numeracy and confidence through activity and routine. A 1:8 teacher–student ratio keeps every child known individually.

Grades 1–5

Primary

A broad foundation across English, Nepali, mathematics, discovery science, social studies, ICT and global perspective — plus CCA (music, dance, fine arts) and physical excellence, at a 1:16 teacher–student ratio.

Grades 6–8

Lower Secondary

Subject specialists, regular assessment and study skills that prepare students for the secondary years ahead.

Our way of education

Sahara Readers & Writers Club

Age-appropriate books, a research-based e-library, and dedicated time for creative writing, calligraphy and reading practice.

ICT Lab

Students build computer skills and digital literacy in a dedicated ICT lab, applying what they learn to everyday tasks.

Performing & fine arts

An in-house school orchestra, ABRSM-affiliated international music exams, Eastern classical music, and fine arts including pottery, sand painting and calligraphy.

P.P.E. assessments

Periodic Performance Evaluations every two to three months track academics, co-curricular growth, values and motor skills — not just exam scores.

Individualized teaching

Based on each student's P.P.E. results, high achievers get extra challenge while others receive close, supportive attention — no child is taught as an average.

Remediation & community service

One-to-one help before and after school for students who need it, alongside community service programmes that build a sense of belonging beyond the classroom.
